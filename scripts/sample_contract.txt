EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 1, 2024, by and between TechCorp Industries, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Company"), and John Michael Smith ("Employee").

1. POSITION AND DUTIES

1.1 Position. The Company hereby employs the Employee as Senior Software Engineer, and the Employee hereby accepts such employment. The Employee shall report directly to the Chief Technology Officer.

1.2 Duties. The Employee shall perform all duties and responsibilities customarily associated with the position of Senior Software Engineer, including but not limited to:
   - Designing, developing, and maintaining software applications
   - Leading technical initiatives and mentoring junior developers
   - Participating in code reviews and architectural decisions
   - Collaborating with cross-functional teams on product development

1.3 Best Efforts. The Employee agrees to devote their full business time, attention, skill, and best efforts to the performance of their duties under this Agreement.

2. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

2.1 Base Salary. The Company shall pay the Employee an annual base salary of $125,000 (One Hundred Twenty-Five Thousand Dollars), payable in accordance with the Company's standard payroll practices, less applicable withholdings and deductions.

2.2 Annual Bonus. The Employee shall be eligible for an annual performance bonus of up to 20% of the base salary, based on individual and company performance metrics determined by the Board of Directors.

2.3 Stock Options. Subject to Board approval, the Employee shall be granted options to purchase 10,000 shares of the Company's common stock at fair market value, vesting over four years with a one-year cliff.

2.4 Benefits. The Employee shall be entitled to participate in all benefit programs offered by the Company to its employees, including:
   - Health, dental, and vision insurance (Company pays 80% of premiums)
   - 401(k) retirement plan with 4% company match
   - Life insurance coverage equal to 1x annual salary
   - Disability insurance (short-term and long-term)
   - Flexible spending accounts (FSA)

2.5 Paid Time Off. The Employee shall receive:
   - 20 days of paid vacation per calendar year
   - 10 paid holidays as designated by the Company
   - 10 days of paid sick leave per calendar year

3. WORK LOCATION AND SCHEDULE

3.1 Work Location. The Employee's primary work location shall be the Company's headquarters at 123 Innovation Drive, San Francisco, CA 94105. The Employee may work remotely up to 3 days per week with supervisor approval.

3.2 Work Hours. Standard work hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM Pacific Time, with a one-hour lunch break. The Employee may be required to work additional hours as necessary to fulfill job responsibilities.

4. TERM AND EMPLOYMENT AT WILL

4.1 Employment At Will. The Employee's employment with the Company is "at-will." Either party may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice, subject to the provisions of Section 5 below.

4.2 Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on January 1, 2024, and continue until terminated pursuant to Section 5.

5. TERMINATION

5.1 Termination for Cause. The Company may terminate the Employee's employment immediately for Cause. "Cause" shall mean:
   - Material breach of this Agreement
   - Gross negligence or willful misconduct
   - Conviction of a felony or crime of moral turpitude
   - Fraud, embezzlement, or misappropriation of Company assets
   - Repeated failure to perform duties after written warning

5.2 Termination Without Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement without Cause by providing thirty (30) days written notice to the other party. The Company may elect to provide pay in lieu of notice.

5.3 Resignation. The Employee may resign at any time by providing thirty (30) days written notice. The Company may waive all or part of the notice period.

5.4 Severance. If the Company terminates the Employee's employment without Cause:
   - The Employee shall receive 3 months of base salary as severance
   - Health insurance coverage shall continue for 3 months (COBRA subsidy)
   - Vested stock options shall remain exercisable for 90 days post-termination

5.5 Return of Property. Upon termination, the Employee shall immediately return all Company property, including laptops, phones, access cards, documents, and confidential information.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Confidential Information. The Employee acknowledges that during employment, they will have access to confidential and proprietary information of the Company ("Confidential Information"), including:
   - Trade secrets and intellectual property
   - Customer lists and customer data
   - Business plans and financial information
   - Software source code and technical specifications
   - Marketing strategies and pricing information

6.2 Non-Disclosure. The Employee agrees to maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information during and after employment. The Employee shall not disclose, publish, or use Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of their duties.

6.3 Duration. The confidentiality obligations under this Section 6 shall survive termination of employment and continue indefinitely.

7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

7.1 Work Product. All inventions, discoveries, designs, developments, improvements, and other works created by the Employee during employment and related to the Company's business ("Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

7.2 Assignment. The Employee hereby assigns and agrees to assign to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to any Work Product.

7.3 Assistance. The Employee agrees to assist the Company in obtaining and enforcing patents, copyrights, and other protections for Work Product.

8. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

8.1 Non-Compete. For a period of twelve (12) months following termination of employment, the Employee agrees not to:
   - Work for or provide services to any direct competitor of the Company
   - Engage in any business activity that competes with the Company's products or services
   - Invest in any competing business (except for less than 1% ownership of publicly traded companies)

8.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees. For a period of twelve (12) months following termination, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, or hire any employee of the Company.

8.3 Non-Solicitation of Customers. For a period of twelve (12) months following termination, the Employee shall not solicit or attempt to solicit any customer or client of the Company with whom the Employee had contact during employment.

8.4 Geographic Scope. The restrictions in this Section 8 shall apply within the United States and any other country where the Company conducts business.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Mediation. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation before a mutually agreed-upon mediator.

9.2 Arbitration. If mediation is unsuccessful, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association. Arbitration shall take place in San Francisco, California.

9.3 Attorneys' Fees. The prevailing party in any arbitration or legal proceeding shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties concerning the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and communications.

10.2 Amendments. This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties.

10.3 Waiver. The failure of either party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not be construed as a waiver of such provision or the right to enforce it at a later time.

10.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

10.5 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California, without regard to conflicts of law principles.

10.6 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by certified mail, overnight courier, or email to the addresses set forth below.

10.7 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.
